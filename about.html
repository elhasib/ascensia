About Ascensia

Built by people who lived the gap.

Every member of the founding team navigated international admissions from an underserved background, on merit scholarships, without a counselor and without a network that could tell them what they were walking into.

Our story

Why we built this.

The members of this team applied to top universities from Egypt, Kenya, and Venezuela. We know what that process looks like without a counselor, without money for consultants, and without a single person in your school who had done it before. The information existed. The financial aid existed. The opportunity was there. What was missing was access to the network and knowledge that makes it possible to actually act on any of it.

Ascensia was not built as a charity. It was built as a correction. The university admissions system in most of the world distributes outcomes that track closely with socioeconomic advantage. That is not a natural law. It is a structural problem, and it is one that a well-designed program can meaningfully push back against, one cohort at a time.

The program is free and will stay free. What we ask in return is seriousness, presence, and eventually, the willingness to become a mentor to whoever comes next. That is the only way this compounds.

What we believe

Five values that are not words on a wall.

These are the things we actually ask of everyone in the program, including ourselves. They are behavioral, not aspirational.

C
Curiosity

We do not pretend to have all the answers. We ask better questions. Our learning is about understanding, not only performance.

P
Perseverance

We finish what we start. Setbacks are part of the process. The willingness to keep going when it gets hard is not optional — it is the whole thing.

I
Integrity

We tell the truth. We do not promise what we cannot deliver. We are people of our word, even when it is easier not to be.

E
Excellence

We hold a high standard. We do the work carefully, not just quickly. The universities we prepare students for hold that same standard, and so do we.

R
Reciprocity

We do not take resources for granted. Someone helped us. We help the next person. That is how a community compounds over time.
